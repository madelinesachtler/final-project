Overview

To begin our analysis, we took a look at game length and how it varied across days of the week. Among Us is commonly played on one’s smartphone, and game length starts and ends with either a victory/loss for the crewmates/imposters or whenever the player chooses to exit the game, not playing further after they are killed. The beautiful thing about Among Us, is that though it can be an intense game of survival and betrayal, the time to actually complete a game can last between two and ten minutes: meaning that it can essentially be played all hours of the day.

In looking at the chart to the right, you can see that though the average game time varies from about 439s (7.3 minutes) and 706s (11.7 minutes), the variance stays pretty constant throughout the day. It appears that all days of the week have the highest lasting games at night, whereas afternoon, evening and morning are all competing for the shortest game length. Our first predications arose from the fact that during the normal working day, which in this case would be from 8am – 5pm, that most individuals would invest in shorter games then they would when they are off work and have more free time. We can see something similar in this graph displayed to the right. Monday morning has the shortest game length at an average of 439s and Tuesday night having the longest average game length of 706s. Sunday shows the highest average against all three times of day, whereas Monday shows the lowest average of all three times of day across the board.
